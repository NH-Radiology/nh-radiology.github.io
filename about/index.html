About

The New Hampshire Radiology Society is a chapter of the American College of Radiology and is an organization dedicated to the socio-economic interests of its members, the advancement of the science of radiology and the radiologic care of patients.

Mission

The New Hampshire Radiology Society is a professional association dedicated to:

  • Advancing the science of radiology
  • Improving radiologic service to patients and the medical community
  • Studying the economics of radiology
  • Encouraging improved and continued education for radiologists
  • Establishing and maintaining high medical and ethical standards in the practice of radiology

Membership Benefits

Membership is open to ACR members who are practicing in the state of New Hampshire and includes radiologists, radiation oncologists and medical physicists.

Government Relations

We represent the field of radiology at the state and local level, monitoring legislation that affects the practice of radiology and provides input on decisions affecting reimbursement at the local level.

National Representation

At the ACR Annual Meeting, our Councilors and Alternate Councilors represent the NH Chapter and have an opportunity to express the needs and interest of our members.

Professional Involvement

Through chapter membership, members know that they are not alone in attempting to find solutions to problems facing radiology today, both at the national and local level.

Resident and Fellows Section

The NH Chapter supports its residents and fellows by sponsoring their representatives to attend the ACR Annual Meeting.

Pathway to ACR Fellowship

Although there are multiple pathways to ACR Fellowship, by providing opportunities for leadership and involvement, the NH Chapter gives members an opportunity for recognition as an ACR Fellow.
